# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-12*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:**  
ROSA is a smart padel infrastructure platform that combines live in-match scoring hardware with post-match analytics and replay intelligence.

**What it does:**  
ROSA gives clubs, tournaments, and players a connected match experience. At entry level it delivers robust, app-free real-time score control; at higher tiers it adds richer display, match replay, player stats, and AI-assisted analysis.

**Product category:**  
Sports tech for padel court operations and performance intelligence.

**Product type:**  
Hardware + software platform (B2B and B2B2C).

**Business model:**  
Device sales for club/tournament infrastructure, with premium software/analytics layers in higher product tiers.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:**  
Padel clubs, academy operators, tournament organizers, and court builders.

**Decision-makers:**  
Club owners, operations managers, tournament directors, and in some cases head coaches.

**Primary use case:**  
Run better matches with professional live scoring and upgraded player/spectator experience.

**Jobs to be done:**  
- Keep match scoring accurate and fast without interrupting play.  
- Deliver professional event presentation for players and spectators.  
- Turn match data and replay into actionable player insights.

**Use cases:**  
- Club daily play and leagues.  
- Tournament operations with referee support.  
- Player performance review and coaching support.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| Club owner/operator | Revenue, differentiation, member retention | Courts feel generic, hard to stand out | A visible premium match experience that attracts and retains players |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:**  
Most padel environments still offer basic scoring with limited post-match intelligence.

**Why alternatives fall short:**  
- Basic scoreboards stop at score display.  
- AI point-solutions lack integrated in-match flow.  
- Fragmented tools create operational friction for clubs.

**What it costs them:**  
Lost differentiation, weaker event quality, lower player engagement, and missed coaching/performance opportunities.

**Emotional tension:**  
Clubs and organizers want a pro-level experience but feel constrained by outdated or disconnected tools.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Basic smart scoreboards for padel/court sports - usually reliable but limited to in-game score display.  
**Secondary:** Video analysis or coaching apps - useful for review but disconnected from court scoring workflow.  
**Indirect:** Manual officiating and analog scoreboards - low cost, low intelligence, inconsistent experience.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**  
- End-to-end experience from in-game control to post-game intelligence.  
- Hardware interaction designed for practical on-court operation.  
- Product ladder from plug-and-play entry to AI-powered analysis.

**How we do it differently:**  
ROSA links real-time scoring infrastructure with match replay and analytics in one product system.

**Why that's better:**  
Users avoid tool fragmentation and get a clear upgrade path as their needs mature.

**Why customers choose us:**  
ROSA improves both operations (during the match) and outcomes (after the match).

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We only need a simple scoreboard." | Start with Core LED now and keep a clear path to Core HD or Vision as demand grows. |
| "Advanced systems are hard to operate." | ROSA is designed around practical, fast match flow with intuitive controls. |
| "Will players actually use the analytics?" | Replay and performance insights tie directly to player motivation and coaching outcomes. |

**Anti-persona:**  
Facilities that do not value match experience upgrades and only optimize for lowest upfront cost.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Existing setups feel basic and undifferentiated.  
**Pull:** Professional presentation plus data-driven improvement.  
**Habit:** Clubs are used to simple/manual routines.  
**Anxiety:** Setup complexity, reliability concerns, and uncertainty about ROI.
